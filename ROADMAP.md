# Tonic Roadmap

Wave 5 turns the Wave 4 utility suite into a contextual recovery platform. The 5.0 implementation includes Recovery Center, Top Shelf, a curated signed provider marketplace, compatibility-gated direct integrations, per-app window rules, six-locale catalogs, local support diagnostics, Sparkle release infrastructure, and Store exclusion audits.

## Commerce and distribution

- Production commerce remains explicitly deferred: StoreKit products, direct licensing, pricing, trials, paywalls, entitlement migration, refunds, family sharing, and purchase restoration.
- `TONIC_COMMERCE` remains inactive. Tonic 5 is fully free and unlocked, and dormant legacy license preferences are left untouched.
- Professional/native linguistic review may refine the six machine-assisted catalogs after internal in-context QA; English remains the fallback.

## Menu Bar platform depth

- Expand the provider SDK with additional reviewed display data types only after schema adoption evidence; providers remain unable to submit arbitrary actions.
- Replace quarantined private adapters with supported public macOS APIs if Apple publishes stable Space identity or foreign status-menu access.
- Expand Top Shelf integrations only when each module has an explicit ambient, permission, persistence, and Store policy.

## Privileged maintenance

- Add any further privileged operation only after it receives a fixed-path threat model, signed protocol case, review UI, receipt schema, recovery path, and Store report-only equivalent.
- Complete release-signing QA for helper upgrade/downgrade, stale registration, wrong-client rejection, and uninstall recovery.

## Capture spike outcome

The offscreen foreign-item image-capture spike is not a reliable universal foundation. Status-item windows that macOS moves offscreen may return no usable pixels, stale content, or a width-only representation depending on owner and OS build. Wave 4 therefore treats imagery as optional and ephemeral and falls back in order to the owning app icon, a known SF Symbol, then a labeled placeholder. Saved layout identity never depends on captured pixels; width-only evidence may inform overflow spacing but is not presented as a faithful preview.
